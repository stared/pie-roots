# Other PIE roots — fertility survey (2026-07-09, source-verified)

A scouting survey of Proto-Indo-European roots, looking for the ones most fertile **in the way \*deru- 'tree' is fertile**: not raw descendant count, but the yield of familiar English words whose descent is invisible — the way *true*, *trust*, *tar* and *druid* hide a tree. Twenty roots surveyed (sixteen in depth, four lightly); every headline claim below carries a per-word solidity grade.

## Sources and method

Verified against the standard references, in this hierarchy:

1. **Watkins**, *The American Heritage Dictionary of Indo-European Roots* — the root→English-derivatives inventory. Used via the live AHD appendix (all 605 roots on one page): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html (per-root anchors cited below).
2. **Pokorny**, *Indogermanisches etymologisches Wörterbuch* (IEW) — branch coverage and entry size. Used via the UT-Austin LRC Indo-European Lexicon (https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master, per-root pages cited) with spot-checks in the archive.org IEW scan. Two LRC caveats: its reflex tables include loan-transmission rows (inflating Romance counts) and Tolkien-invented names (Dernhelm, Samwise, Simbelmynë…), which were excluded from all counts here.
3. **Leiden dictionaries** (archive.org, page numbers cited): de Vaan, *EDL* 2008 (https://archive.org/details/de-vaan-michiel-etymological-dictionary-of-latin); Beekes, *EDG* 2010 (https://archive.org/details/etymological-dictionary-of-greek-2010); Kroonen, *EDPG* 2013 (https://archive.org/details/etymological-dictionary-of-proto-germanic) — the skeptical scrutiny layer, spot-checked on the claims the ranking rests on.
4. Wiktionary and etymonline — first-sweep and per-word link layer only, not authorities. Where they disagree with the specialists, the specialists win and the conflict is recorded.

**Grades** on each word: **SOLID** = chain agreed across sources, no hedge at any link; **HEDGED** = a "perhaps/probably" somewhere in the chain, or a single-source chain; **DISPUTED** = a competing published etymology (named). Watkins is systematically the most inclusive source; Pokorny sits in the middle; Leiden is the most skeptical. A word graded SOLID here survives all three tiers.

## The best verified surprises across all roots

Familiar word + invisible connection + SOLID grade, regardless of root:

**heavy** ('what is held' ← seize) · **defeat** (literally 'to un-do' ← put) · **history**/**story** (one Greek 'knowing one', twice) · **nest** ('where the bird sits down') · **soot** ('what settles') · **weird** (Old English *wyrd* 'Fate', ← turn → 'what comes about') · **fate**/**fairy** ('what is spoken' / the Fates) · **anthem** (← antiphon, 'sounding in answer' ← voice ← speak) · **constable** ('count of the stable' ← stand) · **stoic** (the Painted Porch ← stand) · **confetti** ('things prepared' — the sweets became paper) · **envy** ('looking at with malice') · **ecstasy** ('standing outside oneself') · **second** ('the following one', borrowed because Old English *other* was ambiguous) · **tradition**/**treason** (one 'handing over', twice) · **welcome** ('wished-for comer', not 'well + come') · **have** (unrelated to Latin *habēre*; true cousin is *capere* 'seize') · **chafe** ('make warm' ← calefacere) · **ersatz** ('set in another's place' ← sit) · **abandon** ('at someone's ban' ← speak) · **color** ('a covering' — de Vaan p. 126–7) · **caitiff** (doublet of captive) · **foreclose** (not 'fore-': 'shut outside the door').

Near-misses worth knowing: **money** (Juno Monēta 'the Admonisher' — the mint step is solid, the 'warn' step hedged in every source); **die** (the cube, 'what is given by Fortune' — etymonline hedges); **penguin** (Welsh 'white head' with 'white' ← 'visible' — disputed twice over); **starboard** ('steering side', but steer ← stand is "phonetically impossible" per Wiktionary's sources); **scarlet** (← sealed cloth ← signum — two disputed links); **apocalypse** ('un-covering' — Beekes rules the Greek verb Pre-Greek, so it leaves the PIE family entirely).

## 1. \*weyd- 'to see' → 'to know' (perfect \*wóyde 'has seen')

Watkins **weid-** (~61 Eng. derivatives): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR124900 · Pokorny **2. u̯(e)id-**, IEW 1125–27: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/2115 · de Vaan videō pp. 676–7 (*uid-eh₁-*, LIV *ueid-*); Beekes ἵστωρ pp. 602–3, ᾍδης p. 34; Kroonen \*witan- p. 589.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **history** and **story** — SOLID. Greek ἵστωρ 'the knowing one' ← \*wéydtōr 'one who has seen' (Beekes pp. 602–3, unhedged, dismissing the rival 'sit' derivation) → Latin historia → Old French; *story* is *historia* with the first syllable worn off in Anglo-Norman. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/story
- **envy** — SOLID. Latin invidēre 'to look upon with malice'; the hostile look became the feeling. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/envy
- **idea**, **idol**, **idyll** — SOLID. Greek εἶδος 'the seen form': abstracted into the mental form; hardened into the worshipped image; diminutive εἰδύλλιον 'little picture' → pastoral scene. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/idea
- **wizard**, **wiseacre** — SOLID / HEDGED-in-form-only. 'The knowing one' as sage-turned-sorcerer; wiseacre via Middle Dutch wijssegger 'soothsayer' (the -acre is folk-etymology). https://www.etymonline.com/word/wiseacre
- **twit** — SOLID (verb). Old English ætwītan 'to see (a fault) in, reproach'; the noun 'fool' is later. https://www.etymonline.com/word/twit
- **visa** — SOLID. Latin *charta vīsa*, 'a paper that has been seen'. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/visa
- **advice**, **prudent** (← prōvidēns 'foreseeing'), **provide/purvey** (doublet), **interview**, **survey**, **review**, **clairvoyant** — SOLID Latin vidēre band. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/prudent
- **guide**, **guy** (the rope only) — HEDGED transmission (Frankish \*wītan 'show the way'); the fellow-sense *guy* does NOT belong (← the name Guy ← Germanic 'wood'). https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/guy
- **Hades** — SOLID per Beekes: \*n̥-wid- 'the Unseen One' ("seems the correct one", p. 34); Watkins says "perhaps". A rare case where Leiden is more confident than Watkins.
- **Veda** — SOLID; Sanskrit 'knowledge', cognate with *wit*. **druid** — the 'knower' half is \*weyd- on either reading of the first element. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Veda
- **penguin**, **colcannon** — DISPUTED. Welsh pen gwyn 'white head' / Irish 'white-headed cabbage': the word-level etymologies are contested AND Proto-Celtic \*windos 'white' ← \*weyd- is only "perhaps" (Matasović declines). Charming, not load-bearing. https://www.etymonline.com/word/penguin

Transparent crowd (~25–30, count only): vision, video, view, visit, evident, supervise, television, wit, wise, wisdom, eidetic, -oid…

Scale: large (IEW ~2.5 pp.; 249 LRC rows; every branch except Anatolian/Tocharian/Albanian). Big but curatable.

## 2. \*bʰeh₂- 'to speak' (homophonous twin: \*bʰeh₂- 'to shine')

Watkins **bhā-²** (~48): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR009400 · Pokorny **2. bhā-**, IEW 105–06: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0180 (he splits speak/shine exactly as moderns do) · de Vaan for/fātum/fāma pp. 231, fateor p. 204; Beekes φημί pp. 1566–7, φωνή pp. 1601–2; Kroonen \*bannan- p. 52.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **fate** — SOLID. Latin fātum 'that which has been spoken' (de Vaan p. 231, LIV 2.\*bʰeh₂-); the divine pronouncement became destiny — and displaced native *wyrd* (see \*wert-). https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/fate
- **fairy** — SOLID. Old French fae ← Latin Fāta 'the Fates' ← fātum: beings named for speaking destinies. https://www.etymonline.com/word/fairy
- **infant**, **infantry** — SOLID. Latin īnfāns 'not able to speak'; the non-speakers became the babies, then the foot-soldiers too green for cavalry. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/infantry
- **ban, banns, banal, abandon, contraband** — SOLID. Kroonen (p. 52) derives \*bannan- 'proclaim' from \*bʰeh₂- via a nasal present (cognate Skt bhánati): proclamation → prohibition; 'held under the feudal ban, common to all' → banal 'commonplace'; *a bandon* 'at someone's jurisdiction' → abandon; 'against the proclamation' → contraband. **bandit** — HEDGED (form contaminated by Gothic bandwjan 'signal'). https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/abandon
- **blame** — SOLID as doublet of **blaspheme** (Late Latin blasphēmāre; the -phēm- half is φημί; the blas- element unexplained per Beekes). https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/blame
- **anthem** — SOLID. Old English antefn ← antiphōna 'sounding in answer' ← φωνή — and Beekes (pp. 1601–2) defends φωνή ← \*bʰoh₂-neh₂ against Frisk's doubt, so the whole phone/symphony/euphemism family genuinely belongs. https://www.etymonline.com/word/anthem
- **fame**, **boon** ('the spoken plea' ← Old Norse bón), **prophet** ('who speaks for a god'), **nefarious** ('what divine law does not permit spoken'), **preface**, **ineffable**, **affable**, **aphasia** — SOLID band. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/nefarious
- **confess**, **professor** — HEDGED at the morphology only: de Vaan (p. 204) keeps fateor in the root (LIV 2.\*bʰeh₂-) but flags the derivational path as debated.
- **fandango**, **bee** (the work-party) — DISPUTED; Watkins lists both, the other sources don't support them. Dropped.

The **'shine' twin** (Watkins bhā-¹, Pokorny 1. bhā-, IEW 104–05) yields photo-, phase, phenomenon, epiphany, fantasy, phantom, beacon/beckon — a ready-made look-alike counterpart, structurally the same situation as \*h₂stḗr's \*ster- 'stiff' neighbors.

Transparent crowd (~15–20): fatal, fabulous, famous, infamous, prophecy, euphemism, telephone, symphony, phonetic…

Scale: medium (IEW ~1.5 pp.; 241 LRC rows but mostly loan-chains; no Celtic/Baltic/Iranian branch). **The rare high-surprise family that is fully mappable.**

## 3. \*sed- 'to sit'

Watkins **sed-** (~60): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR096300 · Pokorny **sed-**, IEW 884–87: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/1658 (356 LRC rows — third-largest) · de Vaan sedeō pp. 551–2, nīdus pp. 408–9; Beekes ἕζομαι pp. 375–6; Kroonen \*nista- p. 391, \*stōla- p. 481 (which he notes may be \*sed- rather than \*steh₂-!).

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **nest** — SOLID everywhere. \*ni-sd-ós 'where [the bird] sits down' (de Vaan pp. 408–9; Kroonen p. 391: "generally analyzed" as \*ni 'down' + \*sed-). https://www.etymonline.com/word/*sed-
- **soot** — SOLID. Proto-Germanic \*sōtam, 'what settles'. https://www.etymonline.com/word/soot
- **size** — SOLID. Old French sise ← assise 'court session, assessment' ← assidēre 'sit beside as judge': the court's sitting fixed regulated amounts, whence magnitude. https://www.etymonline.com/word/size
- **siege** ('the army sitting down before a fortress'), **see** (bishop's, ← sēdēs 'seat'), **chair**/**cathedral** (Greek καθέδρα 'a sitting-down'; ἕδρα ← \*sed- per Beekes) — all SOLID. https://www.etymonline.com/word/*sed-
- **ersatz** — SOLID. German ersetzen ← 'make sit, put in another's place' (causative \*sod-éye-). https://www.etymonline.com/word/ersatz
- **banshee** — SOLID. Irish bean sídhe 'woman of the fairy mound'; síd 'otherworld seat'. https://www.etymonline.com/word/banshee
- **eyas** (nestling hawk, rebracketed from "a nyas" ← nīdus), **cosset** ('cot-sitter' → pet lamb → pamper), **tanist** — HEDGED. **soil** — HEDGED (a three-word merger in Old French; only one strand from solium 'seat'). **niche** — DISPUTED (nest-word vs. Italian seashell; both problematic). **nick** — unsupported; dropped (both sources: unknown origin). https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/eyas
- Gems beyond English: **piezo**electric ('sit-upon' pressure, Greek πιέζειν ← \*pi-sed-); **Upanishad** ('a sitting near the teacher') and **Eisteddfod** ('a session') — one root serving Hindu scripture and Welsh poetry.

Transparent crowd (~40–50): sit, seat, settle, saddle (hedged), session, sediment, preside, reside, possess, obsess, assess, supersede, -hedron…

Scale: very large (IEW ~3.5 pp.) — needs heavy curation, but the surprise tier is compact and almost entirely SOLID.

## 4. \*dʰeh₁- 'to put, place, do'

Watkins **dhē-** (~148 — his largest inventory of the sixteen): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR021100 · Pokorny **2. dhē-**, IEW 235–39: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0376 (591 LRC rows; all branches except Albanian).

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **defeat** — SOLID. Old French desfaire 'to UN-do' ← facere: ruin → overcome. https://www.etymonline.com/word/defeat
- **confetti** — SOLID. Italian confetto 'sugared almond' ('thing prepared' ← cōnficere); carnival sweets thrown at crowds gave way to the paper symbolizing them. https://www.etymonline.com/word/confetti
- **chafe** — SOLID. calefacere 'make warm' → warmth by rubbing → the abrasion. https://www.etymonline.com/word/chafe
- **surfeit** ('a thing over-done'), **hacienda** (Latin facienda 'things needing doing' — cf. agenda), **sconce** (the wall light: absconsus 'hidden' ← condere 'put away') — SOLID. https://www.etymonline.com/word/hacienda
- **credit**, **creed** — SOLID. Latin crēdere ← \*kerd-dʰeh₁- 'to put one's heart', a two-word PIE poem surviving in Latin, Old Irish cretim, and Sanskrit śrad-dhā. https://www.etymonline.com/word/credit
- **doom**, **deem**, **-dom** — SOLID. Proto-Germanic \*dōmaz 'what is set down' = law, judgment (Doomsday = judgment day); the darkness is post-1600. https://www.etymonline.com/word/doom
- **boutique / bodega / apothecary** — SOLID. One Greek ἀποθήκη 'place where things are put away', three arrivals. https://www.etymonline.com/word/boutique
- **fetish** (facticius 'made by art' → Portuguese charm-word), **chauffeur** ('stoker', calefacere again), **fashion** ('a making'), **office**, **profit**, **counterfeit**, **forfeit** — SOLID band. **add** — SOLID but *belongs here, not under 'give'*: both de Vaan's framework and etymonline put Latin -dere compounds under \*dʰeh₁- 'put' (Watkins' assignment of *add* to \*dō- is the minority reading of the Latin compound merger). **abdomen** ('the part put away') — DISPUTED: de Vaan calls the abdere derivation "unfounded". **purdah** — DISPUTED at the PIE level. https://www.etymonline.com/word/add
- Doom-loop gem: **Ahura Mazda** is arguably 'mind-placer' — \*men- + \*dʰeh₁- in one god's name (HEDGED; Wiktionary calls the formation post-PIE).

Transparent crowd: the -fy/-fic/-faction legion plus the Greek thesis family (~100+): fact, factory, effect, perfect, sacrifice, satisfy, thesis, theme, synthesis, hypothesis, parenthesis, epithet…

Scale: huge (591 LRC rows; second-largest). The single best surprises of the whole survey live here, but the family can only be sampled, never mapped whole.

## 5. \*kap- 'to seize, grasp'

Watkins **kap-** (~53): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR046300 · Pokorny **kap-**, IEW 527–28 — his own gloss carries the question-mark: 'to cop, grab, grasp; have?': https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0839 · de Vaan capiō pp. 89–90; Kroonen \*habēn-/\*hafjan- pp. 197–8.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **have** — SOLID within the family, with a structural caveat. Proto-Germanic \*habēn- ← \*kh₂p-éh₁- (Kroonen p. 197); de Vaan (p. 90): "Latin habeo and Go. giban require \*gʰa/ebʰ-" — i.e. English *have* and Latin *habēre* mean the same, look the same, and are unrelated; *have*'s true Latin cousin is capere 'seize'. Caveat: de Vaan flags the root as possibly non-IE, Kroonen tags it "EUR" (European only) — solid family, uncertain pedigree. https://www.etymonline.com/word/have
- **heavy** — SOLID. \*habīgaz 'containing something, having weight'. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/heavy
- **heave** — SOLID (\*kapyéti 'is seizing' → lifting). **cash** — SOLID (Latin capsa 'box, that which holds' → French caisse 'money box' → the money inside). **chase**/**catch** — SOLID doublet (one Vulgar Latin \*captiare, split by an Old French dialect boundary). **cable** (capulum 'lasso'), **prince** (primus + capere, 'who takes first place'), **caitiff** (doublet of captive: 'captive' → 'wretch') — SOLID. https://www.etymonline.com/word/cash
- **cater** — HEDGED (captāre vs. a caput-derivation). **behoove** — HEDGED ('what one takes up for oneself'). **cop** (the policeman) — DISPUTED (French caper ← capere vs. Dutch kapen; only the first reaches this root). **hawk** (the bird as 'seizer') — DISPUTED: Kroonen calls the correspondences "too irregular", likely a Wanderwort. https://www.etymonline.com/word/cop

Transparent crowd (~25–30): capture, accept, receive, deceive, conceive, occupy, participate, recipe, prey, purchase…

Scale: large (242 LRC rows, mostly the Latin borrowing machinery). Bonus fact: PIE probably had no verb 'to have' at all — possession was expressed as 'to me is'.

## 6. \*steh₂- 'to stand'

Watkins **stā-** (~131): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR109800 · Pokorny **stā-**, IEW 1004–10 — the largest entry of the sixteen (617 LRC rows, all fifteen branch families): https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/1873.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **constable** — SOLID. Late Latin *comes stabulī* 'count of the stable' (stabulum 'standing-place'): the imperial stable officer rose to great household officer, then sank to policeman. https://www.etymonline.com/word/constable
- **stoic** — SOLID. The Stoa Poikilē, the 'Painted Porch' where Zeno taught: a colonnade ('standing thing') → a school → a temperament. https://www.etymonline.com/word/stoic
- **ecstasy** ('standing outside oneself'), **cost** (constāre 'stand at a price'), **oust** (obstāre 'stand against'), **stamina** (the Fates' warp-threads), **shtetl** (doublet of *stead*), **post** (the pole; both parses run through \*steh₂-), **étagère**, **-stan** (Afghanistan: 'where one stands', parallel formation to *state*) — SOLID band. https://www.etymonline.com/word/ecstasy
- **stool** — SOLID with a lovely arc (Old English 'throne' → backless seat → privy → the medical sense) and one footnote: Kroonen (p. 481) reports an alternative derivation from \*sed- 'sit' — the stool may belong to sitting rather than standing.
- **understand** — HEDGED semantics ('probably "stand in the midst of"'). **starboard**/**steer** — DISPUTED: Watkins' extended \*stāu- is called "phonetically impossible" by Wiktionary's sources, which prefer \*(s)twerH- 'stir'. **Taurus** — DISPUTED (Watkins' extension vs. de Vaan/Klein's Semitic loan). https://www.etymonline.com/word/starboard
- **peevish** — dropped: unattached to any root with confidence (despite Watkins filing it under \*wer-).

Transparent crowd (100+): stand, state, station, statue, status, system, exist, insist, institute, constitute, superstition, solstice, armistice, establish, stage, stanza…

Scale: the largest of all — by IEW pages, LRC rows, and Watkins count alike. Sample-only.

## 7. \*wert- 'to turn' (Pokorny files it inside \*wer-)

Watkins **wer-²** (~85 across the whole umbrella): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR127600 · Pokorny **3h. u̯er-t-** inside the eight-page **3. u̯er-** article, IEW 1156–58: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/2163 · de Vaan vertō pp. 666–7; Kroonen \*werþa- p. 581.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **weird** — SOLID. Old English wyrd 'Fate', literally 'that which comes about' (turn → become, as in "turn into"); kept alive by the Scots 'weird sisters' (the Norns), reached Shakespeare's witches, diluted by 19c. theatre into 'strange'. https://www.etymonline.com/word/weird
- **-ward** ('turned in the direction of'), **verse** (the plowman's turn at the furrow's end) and **prose** (prōrsus '(moving) straight ahead' — speech that doesn't turn), **divorce** ('a turning apart'), **anniversary**, **universe** — SOLID Latin band (vertō ← \*uert-, de Vaan pp. 666–7). https://www.etymonline.com/word/prose
- **worth**, **worship** — LOST: Kroonen (p. 581) rules \*werþa- "of unknown origin", Germanic-only; the 'turned toward → equivalent' story doesn't survive scrutiny. (The verb \*werþan- 'to become' ← \*wert- is solid; the adjective is not derived from it in Kroonen.)
- **worm**/**vermin**/**vermicelli** ('the twisters') — belong to sister extensions of the \*wer- umbrella, not \*wert- proper; keep the layers distinct (Watkins lumps, Pokorny sub-divides, Wiktionary splits).

Transparent crowd (30+): version, convert, invert, revert, adverse, vertigo, vortex, controversy, introvert…

Scale: mid-size as \*wert- proper; the whole \*wer- complex is one of Pokorny's biggest articles. Structural caveat everywhere: no two references slice this family the same way.

## 8. \*deh₃- 'to give'

Watkins **dō-** (~35): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR025600 · Pokorny **dō-**, IEW 223–26: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0362 — a 3.5-page article but only 125 LRC rows: **no Celtic branch and no native Germanic reflex at all**; every English member is a loan.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **tradition / treason / traitor / betray** — SOLID as a set: one Latin verb, trādere 'hand over', four arrivals; the senses still overlapped in the 1450s. (Root-assignment footnote: Wiktionary derives trādō from dō 'give'; etymonline files the -dere compounds under 'put' — the two roots merged formally in Latin compounds. Either way the English quadruplet is one Latin word.) https://www.etymonline.com/word/treason
- **anecdote** — SOLID. Greek anékdota 'things not given out': Procopius' unpublished court gossip → revelations → dinner-party story. https://www.etymonline.com/word/anecdote
- **date**, **data** (Roman letters dated "datum Romae"), **rent**/**render**/**surrender** (reddere 'give back'), **pardon** ('give completely'), **vend** (vēnum dare 'give for sale'), **dose**/**antidote**, **dacha** ('a granted plot'), **samizdat** ('self-given-out' — clandestine publishing) — SOLID band. https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/дача
- **die** (the gaming cube, 'what is given by Fortune') — HEDGED (etymonline: "perhaps"). **add** — REMOVED to \*dʰeh₁- (see §4). **Thermidor** (Greek dōron 'gift' in the revolutionary month behind lobster thermidor) — SOLID, single-source. **Dorothy** = **Theodora** reversed, both 'gift of God'; **Pandora** 'all-gifts'.

Transparent crowd (~10): donate, donor, dative, dowry, endow, condone…

Scale: medium — and the historical curiosity is structural: the 'give' root died out in Germanic and Celtic entirely, so English has it only through borrowing. Pokorny even keeps Hittite dā- 'TAKE' inside this entry — the give/take polarity moderns still argue about.

## 9. \*men- 'to think'

Watkins **men-¹** (~47): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR070500 · Pokorny **3. men-**, IEW 726–28 (maximal branch spread — 13 families incl. Hittite): https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/1282 · Beekes Μοῦσα pp. 972–3.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **monster** — SOLID. Latin mōnstrum 'divine omen' ← monēre 'remind, warn': a monster was a message before it was a creature; **demonstrate**, **muster**, **summon**, **premonition** are the same 'making-think'. https://www.etymonline.com/word/monster
- **money** / **mint** — HEDGED at one step, delightful anyway: coins struck at the temple of Jūnō Monēta gave Latin its mint-word, and English took it twice, a millennium apart (Old English mynet → mint; Old French → money). The Monēta ← monēre 'the Admonisher' step is hedged in every source consulted. https://www.etymonline.com/word/mint
- **mandarin** — SOLID (Sanskrit mantrin- 'adviser' via Malay and Portuguese; the orange from the robes). **mantis** ('the seer'; same mantis in necromancy's -mancy), **automatic** ('thinking for itself'), **amnesty**/**amnesia** (a legal not-remembering), **minnesinger** (Old High German minna 'loving remembrance'), **Ahriman** (Avestan 'destructive spirit', mainiiu 'mind/spirit') — SOLID band. https://www.etymonline.com/word/mandarin
- **Muse → music, museum, mosaic** — LOST per Beekes (pp. 972–3): Μοῦσα "cannot be directly related to \*men-"; at best \*men(s)-dʰh₁- (the μανθάνω root), possibly Pre-Greek. Watkins includes the whole set; the skeptical tier removes it.
- **memory** — conflicted (Wiktionary's \*men- vs. etymonline's \*(s)mer-); keep out.

Transparent crowd (~20): mind, mental, mention, memento, monitor, monument, admonish, reminisce, mnemonic, mantra, mania…

Scale: large, with the widest branch spread after \*steh₂-/\*dʰwer-. The abstract root meaning blunts the concrete-to-abstract contrast that powers the \*deru- effect.

## 10. \*gʷem- 'to come, step'

Watkins **gwā-/gwem-** (~53): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR040850 · Pokorny **gu̯ā-/gu̯em-**, IEW 463–65: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0701 — Pokorny merges what Wiktionary/LIV split into \*gʷem- (come, veniō) and \*gʷeh₂- (Greek步 basis words); the Greek arm rides on the merged reading.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **welcome** — SOLID. Old English wilcuma 'a wished-for comer' (willa 'desire' + cuma 'guest'); *well* muscled in by folk-etymology. https://www.etymonline.com/word/welcome
- **coven / convent / covenant** — SOLID triplets of convenīre 'come together': nuns, witches, contracts. https://www.etymonline.com/word/coven
- **adventure** ('a thing about to happen': chance → risk → excitement), **parvenu** ('one who has arrived'), **souvenir**, **avenue**, **revenue**, **event** — SOLID Latin band. https://www.etymonline.com/word/parvenu
- **diabetes** — SOLID (Greek 'the passer-through, siphon' — Aretaeus' name for the disease), **base/basis** ('a stepping'), **acrobat** ('tiptoe-goer') — via the Greek arm (\*gʷeh₂- on the split reading). **bema**, **Durga** — technically \*gʷeh₂-, single-source; keep as footnotes. **juggernaut** — SOLID chain (Jagannātha 'lord of the moving world'), and the crushing-cart story is apocryphal. https://www.etymonline.com/word/diabetes

Transparent crowd (~25): come, become, income, advent, venue, convene, convenient, prevent, invent, intervene…

Scale: large. No disputes on the headline words; the only caveat is the lumper/splitter line through the middle of the family.

## 11. \*speḱ- 'to observe, look'

Watkins **spek-** (~38): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR107400 · Pokorny **spek̑-**, IEW 984–85: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/1831 (LRC's exotic-looking branch rows are mostly episkopos loans; Pokorny's own entry opens with Sanskrit spáśati).

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **spice** — SOLID. Latin speciēs 'appearance' → 'kind' → 'kind of merchandise' → the aromatic kind; spice and species are one word. https://www.etymonline.com/word/spice

## 12. \*ḱel- 'to cover, conceal' — demoted after Leiden scrutiny

Watkins **kel-¹** (~34): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR048100 · Pokorny **4. k̑el-**, IEW 553–54: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0893 · de Vaan cēlō p. 106, color pp. 126–7; Beekes καλύπτω pp. 628–9; Kroonen \*haljō- p. 204 (no \*hallō- entry).

What survives, graded:

- **color** — SOLID, and Leiden-confirmed: de Vaan (pp. 126–7) derives color ← \*ḱel-os 'covering', "shifted its semantics from 'covering' to 'colour'". The best word in the family. https://www.etymonline.com/word/color
- **cell / cellar** (cella 'covered storeroom'), **conceal**, **occult**, **clandestine** (clam 'secretly'), **supercilious** (the eyebrow above the eye-covering cilium), **ceiling** (Old French celer 'panel/conceal', with caelum interference), **helmet** — SOLID band, all in the Latin/Germanic core. https://www.etymonline.com/word/supercilious
- **hell** — HEDGED: Kroonen p. 204 says only "possibly" \*ḱel-. **hall** — WEAKENED: Watkins and Pokorny list it, but Kroonen has no \*hallō- entry at all, so the beloved hell/hall sibling pair rests on the older tier only.
- **apocalypse, eucalyptus, Calypso** — LOST per Beekes (pp. 628–9): καλύπτω is "clearly Pre-Greek" — the whole Greek arm leaves the PIE family. **hole, hollow, hull, holster** — Watkins includes, Wiktionary/Kroonen doubt; keep out or hedge.

Scale: medium. Still charming, but scrutiny cost it its Greek arm and half its Germanic poster-pair — the sharpest demotion in the survey, and a caution about building on Watkins alone.

## 13. \*sekʷ- 'to follow'

Watkins **sekʷ-¹** (~43): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR096900 · Pokorny **1. seku̯-**, IEW 896–97: https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/1673 (his 2. seku̯- 'see, say' is a separate etymon — see/say/sight do NOT belong here) · de Vaan socius pp. 569–70, sequor pp. 555–6, signum p. 563.

Unexpected derivatives, graded:

- **second** — SOLID (de Vaan pp. 555–6: secundus \*sekʷo-ndo- 'following', "came to be used as the ordinal"). Borrowed because Old English *other* was ambiguous; the time unit is *secunda pars minuta* of the hour. https://www.etymonline.com/word/second

Watkins **bher-¹** (~50): https://www.ahdictionary.com/word/indoeurop.html#IR012400 · Pokorny **1. bher-**, IEW 128–32 (316 LRC rows; 14 branch families; SEVEN homophonous bher- roots stacked in IEW 128–138): https://lrc.la.utexas.edu/lex/master/0229.

## Cross-source conflict register (methodology)

The systematic pattern, confirmed across all sixteen roots: **Watkins (AHD) is the most inclusive; Pokorny is close behind with more structural splits; the Leiden dictionaries are the most skeptical.** Wiktionary usually follows Leiden; etymonline usually follows Watkins. Specific verdicts recorded above where they change the picture:

- Leiden REMOVES from their Watkins families: apocalypse/eucalyptus/Calypso (Pre-Greek, Beekes 628–9), Muse/music/museum/mosaic (Beekes 972–3), worth/worship (Kroonen 581), company/pantry's PIE step (de Vaan 443), hall (absent from Kroonen), abdomen (de Vaan: "unfounded").
- Leiden CONFIRMS against expectation: Hades = 'the Unseen' (Beekes 34, unhedged), color ← 'covering' (de Vaan 126–7), φωνή ← 'speak' (Beekes 1601–2), ḫistōr/history (Beekes 602–3), nest (everyone), bannan-/ban/abandon (Kroonen 52).
- Structural splits to respect: Pokorny's 2. seku̯- 'see/say' vs 1. seku̯- 'follow' (see/say/sight are NOT follow-words); \*gʷem- vs \*gʷeh₂- (the Greek basis/diabetes arm); \*wert- inside \*wer-; feed vs protect in \*peh₂-; the Latin -dere compound merger between 'give' and 'put' (add, tradition).
- Watkins assignments with no support elsewhere: nick (\*sed-), burly (\*bʰer-), peevish, fandango, bee¹ — dropped from all tiers above.
- de Vaan on **sīdus** (pp. 562–3), incidentally to this survey: the old 'shine' connection is "not very specific"; he ends in confessed speculation — relevant to the star project's Latin material.
