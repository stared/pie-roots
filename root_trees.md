## \*bʰeh₂- 'to speak'

```
*bʰeh₂- (PIE) 'to speak, say'
├── fōr, fārī (Latin) 'to speak' [via Proto-Italic *fāōr]
│   ├── fātum (Latin) 'thing spoken; destiny' → fatal, fatality
│   │   ├── fāta (plural) 'the Fates' → fate; → fae (Old French) → fay, faerie → fairy
│   │   └── fado (Portuguese) 'fate; song genre' → fado
│   ├── fāma (Latin) 'talk, report, renown'
│   │   └── fame, famous, defame, infamous, infamy
│   ├── fābula (Latin) 'talk, story'
│   │   ├── fable, fabulous, confabulate
│   │   └── ? fib (clipped from fible-fable — folk-etymological chain)
│   ├── īnfāns (Latin) 'non-speaking (one); child'
│   │   ├── infant, infancy
│   │   ├── infanteria (Italian) 'the youths, foot-soldiers' → infantry
│   │   └── infanta, infante (Spanish/Portuguese loans)
│   ├── affārī → affābilis → affable
│   ├── effārī → ineffābilis → ineffable
│   ├── praefārī → praefātiō → preface
│   └── (see also root-formations fās, fatērī below — parallel to fārī, not from it)
├── ? fās 'divine utterance, right' (root-formation; Beekes: "no convincing etymology") → nefās → nefārius → nefarious
├── fatērī 'to acknowledge' [*bʰh₂-tó-s; a root-formation parallel to fārī]
│   ├── cōnfitērī → confess, confession
│   └── profitērī → profess, professor, profession
├── φημί (Ancient Greek) 'I say'
│   ├── προφήτης 'spokesman' → prophet, prophecy, prophesy
│   ├── εὔφημος 'auspicious of speech' → euphemism
│   ├── βλάσφημος 'evil-speaking'
│   │   ├── blaspheme, blasphemy
│   │   └── blasphemāre (Late Latin) → blasmer (Old French) → blame
│   ├── φάσις 'utterance' [NOT 'phase' — that φάσις is the 'shine' twin]
│   │   ├── ἀφασία → aphasia
│   │   └── ἀπόφασις → apophasis, apophatic
│   ├── φήμη 'report' → Πολύφημος 'much-famed' → Polyphemus
│   └── φωνή 'voice, sound' [o-grade *bʰoh₂-néh₂]
│       ├── phone, phoneme, phonetic(s), phonology
│       ├── telephone, microphone, megaphone, saxophone, xylophone…
│       ├── symphony, euphony, cacophony, polyphony
│       └── ἀντίφωνα 'answering voices' → antiphona (Late Latin)
│           ├── antiphon (learned re-borrowing)
│           └── antefn (Old English) → anthem (early-loan doublet)
├── *bʰh₂-néw-ti → *bannaną (Proto-Germanic) 'to proclaim, summon'
│   ├── bannan (Old English) → ban, banns (of marriage)
│   ├── *bannjan (Frankish, causative verb)
│   │   ├── banir (Old French) → banish
│   │   └── bannīre (Late Latin) → bandīre/bandire (Italian; -d- after Gothic bandwjan 'signal') → bandito → ? bandit
│   └── *ban (Frankish noun) 'command under penalty'
│       ├── ban (Old French) 'feudal proclamation/jurisdiction' → banal ('of compulsory feudal service' → 'common to all')
│       │   └── bandon 'jurisdiction' → a bandon 'at (someone's) disposal' → abandon
│       └── bannum (Medieval Latin) 'proclamation'
│           └── bando → contrabando (older Italian) 'against the proclamation' → contraband
├── *bʰeh₂-ni-s
│   ├── *bōniz (Proto-Germanic) 'prayer, petition' → bón (Old Norse) → boon
│   ├── ban (Old Armenian) 'word, thing' — no English yield
│   └── basnь (Proto-Slavic) 'fable, spell' — no English yield
├── bhánati (Sanskrit) 'speaks' — no English yield
└── bhāṣā (Sanskrit) 'speech, language' — no English yield
```

**Topology.** ~40 curated English words + ~30 ancestors ≈ **70–85 nodes** — chart-scale. Three fat arms of comparable size (Latin ~22 English words, Greek ~24, Germanic ~8) plus three stub arms that die without English (Armenian, Balto-Slavic, Indo-Iranian). Learned Greek is shallow (3–4 steps); the Germanic ban-family loops are deep (root → Proto-Germanic → Frankish → Old French/Medieval Latin → Italian → English = 6–7 steps). **~7 independent routes into English**: native Old English (ban), Old Norse (boon), Germanic→Frankish→Old French (banal, abandon), Germanic→Medieval Latin→Italian (contraband), Latin→Old French (fable, fame, fate…), learned Latin (nefarious, confess), Greek learned (phone family) with sub-routes Greek→Latin→Old French (blame) and Greek→Late Latin→Old English (anthem — the tree's oldest borrowing).

**False twin appendix** — homophonous \*bʰeh₂- 'to shine' (same Wiktionary page): photo-, phosphorus, phase, phenomenon, epiphany, diaphanous, emphasis, fantasy, fancy, phantom, hierophant, sycophant, cellophane, beacon, beckon, ? banner. Every 'shine' word is a potential trap: *phase* vs *aphasia*, *emphasis* vs *apophasis*, *phenomenon* vs *phoneme* — identical-looking Greek stems from two different roots.

**Hook**: a three-armed tree where each arm smuggles speech into English by a different trick — Latin as destiny (fate = 'what has been said'), Greek as sound, Germanic as command — with a built-in impostor root whose descendants are indistinguishable from the real ones without the etymology.

Source: https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Indo-European/bʰeh₂- · https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Reconstruction:Proto-Germanic/bannaną

## *bʰuH- 'to become, grow, dwell'

```
*bʰuH- 'become, grow, appear'
├─ Germanic 'dwell/become'
│  ├─ OE bēon ────────────── be, been (+ forebear)
│  ├─ *buþlą 'dwelling' ───── build
│  ├─ OE būr 'dwelling' ───── bower, byre
│  ├─ *būraz 'dweller' ────── boor (Boer, Bauer); ? beam ('living tree' = 'growing thing')
│  ├─ OE nēah-gebūr 'near-dweller' ── neighbor
│  ├─ ON búa 'dwell'
│  │   ├─ búð ────────────── booth
│  │   ├─ húsbóndi 'house-dweller/master' ── husband (→ bond, bondage)
│  │   └─ býr 'settlement' ── ? bylaw ('town-law')
│  └─ German bauen ────────── (Bauhaus)
├─ Italic: futūrus (suppletive future of esse); -bam/-bō endings ── future (? fiat)
└─ Hellenic φύω 'grow'
   ├─ φύσις 'nature' ('growth') ── physics, physio-, physique
   ├─ φυτόν 'plant' ('grown thing') ── phyto-, -phyte, neophyte
   ├─ φῦλον 'tribe' ('grown together') ── phylum, phylo-, phylogeny
   └─ ἔμφυτος 'implanted' → VLat *impotus 'graft' → OE impa 'shoot, graft' ── imp
        (graft → offspring → little devil, 1580s)
```

**Topology.** ~25–30 English leaves + ~12 internal nodes — comfortably chart-scale. Two dense clusters that look unrelated to a lay reader: homely Germanic (bower, booth, neighbor, husband) and learned Greek (physics, phylum, neophyte). **Grammatical story beat (verified)**: English 'to be' is three PIE roots suppleted into one verb — *be* ← \*bʰuH-, *am/is* ← \*h₁es-, *was/were* ← \*h₂wes- 'dwell'.

**Hook**: the root of existence itself — the same seed that gave English *be* also grew *physics*, *future*, *neighbor*, *husband*, and the *imp* (a graft that became a little devil).

**Fertility verdict**: comparable to \*deru- in reach and surprise; no single flagship pair like tree/true, but the be↔physics↔husband spread is strong. Grades: be/build/bower/boor/neighbor/booth/husband/future/physics/phyto-/phylum/imp SOLID; bylaw, beam, fiat hedged.

```
│   ├── exigere 'drive out, weigh'
│   │   ├── exāmen (*exagmen) 'tongue of a balance' → exam, examine
│   │   └── exagium 'a weighing' → essay, assay
│   ├── cōgere 'drive together' → cogent, coagulate; cōgitāre → cogitate

│   └── prōdigere 'drive forth, squander' → prodigal

│   ├── ἀγωγός 'leading' → pedagogue, demagogue, synagogue
│   ├── στρατηγός 'army-leader' → strategy, stratagem  ← NOTE: genuinely *h₂eǵ- (the -egy half)
```

**Hook**: one herder's verb — 'drive the cattle' — smuggled into nearly everything English does at a desk: act, agenda, exam ('the tongue of the balance'), essay ('a weighing'), strategy, ambassador ('the one driven around').

**Star-of-star crossover note**: *strategy* sits in the star's look-alikes chapter as a \*sterh₃- 'spread' word — correct for the strat- half (στρατός 'spread-out army'), but the -egy half is \*h₂eǵ-. Worth a precision check in research.md §5 wording.

**Fertility verdict**: surprise density beats \*deru-, but topologically it's officialese — two classical fans, no native register contrast.

**Fertility verdict**: exceeds the \*deru- benchmark — roughly double the solid English yield, and its disputes are *architectural* (a possible root split, a root merger, a semantic reconstruction) rather than leaf-level. wheel=cycle=chakra as one reduplicated PIE word arriving three ways is a first-rank showpiece.

```
│   ├── Latin lūcus 'grove' (context)
│   └── Skt loká 'open space → WORLD' (context)
├── Latin lūx / lūcēre 'shine'
│   ├── lucid, lucent, elucidate, translucent, pellucid

│   └── ? lustrāre 'light up' → illustrate, illustrious, luster  [or ← lustrum 'purification', three-way dispute]
├── *léwk-s-mn̥ → Latin lūmen
│   └── lumen (the other SI unit), luminous, illuminate, luminary, limn (via manuscript illumination)
├── *lówk-s-neh₂ 'THE SHINING ONE' → Latin lūna
│   ├── lunar, Luna, lunatic (moon-madness), lunette, sublunary
│   └── Proto-Slavic *luna → Russian луна; Armenian lusin (context)
├── Greek λευκός 'white' → leukemia, leukocyte, leuko-
│   ├── λύχνος 'lamp' → ? link (the torch; distinct from chain-link)
│   └── ? λύγξ → lynx ('the bright-eyed one'? — phonetic problems; Beekes: substrate)
├── Hittite lukk-ezzi 'is bright' (oldest attestation)
└── Skt rócate 'shines'; Welsh golau; Russian луч 'ray' (context)
```

**Topology.** ~35–40 English + ~10 context nodes. Unusual shape: not one trunk but a **Latin fan** — lūx, lūmen, lūna, lūstrāre are each mini-trees — plus one homely Germanic arm (light, lea) and one Greek (white).

**Hook — and the star pairing**: \*h₂stḗr names the glowing THING; \*lewk- names the GLOW. Zero node overlap, perfect thematic complement: the star chart owns the stars, \*lewk- owns the Moon (lūna 'the shining one', hence *lunatic*) and the morning star (Lucifer). Same night sky, same palette, complementary semantics — the natural companion piece to star-of-star. The lea/-ley place-names ('light place in the woods') are its *soot*-grade hidden gem, and both SI units of light (lux, lumen) are raw Latin from this root.
