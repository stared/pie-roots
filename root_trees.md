## *bʰuH- 'to become, grow, dwell'

```
*bʰuH- 'become, grow, appear'
├─ Germanic 'dwell/become'
│  ├─ OE bēon ────────────── be, been (+ forebear)
│  ├─ *buþlą 'dwelling' ───── build
│  ├─ OE būr 'dwelling' ───── bower, byre
│  ├─ *būraz 'dweller' ────── boor (Boer, Bauer); ? beam ('living tree' = 'growing thing')
│  ├─ OE nēah-gebūr 'near-dweller' ── neighbor
│  ├─ ON búa 'dwell'
│  │   ├─ búð ────────────── booth
│  │   ├─ húsbóndi 'house-dweller/master' ── husband (→ bond, bondage)
│  │   └─ býr 'settlement' ── ? bylaw ('town-law')
│  └─ German bauen ────────── (Bauhaus)
├─ Italic: futūrus (suppletive future of esse); -bam/-bō endings ── future (? fiat)
└─ Hellenic φύω 'grow'
   ├─ φύσις 'nature' ('growth') ── physics, physio-, physique
   ├─ φυτόν 'plant' ('grown thing') ── phyto-, -phyte, neophyte
   ├─ φῦλον 'tribe' ('grown together') ── phylum, phylo-, phylogeny
   └─ ἔμφυτος 'implanted' → VLat *impotus 'graft' → OE impa 'shoot, graft' ── imp
        (graft → offspring → little devil, 1580s)
```

**Topology.** ~25–30 English leaves + ~12 internal nodes — comfortably chart-scale. Two dense clusters that look unrelated to a lay reader: homely Germanic (bower, booth, neighbor, husband) and learned Greek (physics, phylum, neophyte). **Grammatical story beat (verified)**: English 'to be' is three PIE roots suppleted into one verb — *be* ← \*bʰuH-, *am/is* ← \*h₁es-, *was/were* ← \*h₂wes- 'dwell'.

**Hook**: the root of existence itself — the same seed that gave English *be* also grew *physics*, *future*, *neighbor*, *husband*, and the *imp* (a graft that became a little devil).

**Fertility verdict**: comparable to \*deru- in reach and surprise; no single flagship pair like tree/true, but the be↔physics↔husband spread is strong. Grades: be/build/bower/boor/neighbor/booth/husband/future/physics/phyto-/phylum/imp SOLID; bylaw, beam, fiat hedged.

## *h₂eǵ- 'to drive, lead'

```
*h₂eǵ- 'to drive, lead'
├── Latin agere 'drive (cattle) → do'
│   ├── āctus/agēns/agenda → act, action, agent, agenda, agile, agitate (transparent group)
│   ├── ambigere 'drive both ways' → ambiguous
│   ├── exigere 'drive out, weigh'
│   │   ├── exāmen (*exagmen) 'tongue of a balance' → exam, examine
│   │   └── exagium 'a weighing' → essay, assay
│   ├── cōgere 'drive together' → cogent, coagulate; cōgitāre → cogitate
│   │   ├─? VLat *coacticare → French cacher → cache
│   │   └─? VLat *coactire → Old French esquatir → squat
│   ├── -igāre compounds → navigate, fumigate, castigate, mitigate, litigate; pūrigāre → purge
│   └── prōdigere 'drive forth, squander' → prodigal
├── Greek ἄγω 'lead'
│   ├── ἀγών 'gathering driven together → contest' → agony, antagonist, protagonist
│   ├── ἀγωγός 'leading' → pedagogue, demagogue, synagogue
│   ├── στρατηγός 'army-leader' → strategy, stratagem  ← NOTE: genuinely *h₂eǵ- (the -egy half)
│   └─? ἄξιος 'weighing as much → worthy' → axiom (Beekes tentative)
├── Celtic ambactos 'one driven around, vassal' → Latin ambactus → ambassador, embassy (? German Amt)
├── Sanskrit ájati 'drives' — attestation arm, no English
├─? *h₂éǵros 'field' ('where cattle are driven') → agriculture, agrarian, pilgrim, peregrine; Gmc *akraz → acre
└─? *h₂eḱs- 'axis' → axis, axle (probably a separate root)
```

**Topology.** ~76 English words in Watkins/etymonline; curated ~35–45 nodes. Two giant classical fans (Latin, Greek) + one thin gem arm (Celtic → ambassador); native Germanic essentially absent unless disputed *acre* is admitted — the OPPOSITE profile from \*deru-.

**Hook**: one herder's verb — 'drive the cattle' — smuggled into nearly everything English does at a desk: act, agenda, exam ('the tongue of the balance'), essay ('a weighing'), strategy, ambassador ('the one driven around').

**Star-of-star crossover note**: *strategy* sits in the star's look-alikes chapter as a \*sterh₃- 'spread' word — correct for the strat- half (στρατός 'spread-out army'), but the -egy half is \*h₂eǵ-. Worth a precision check in research.md §5 wording.

**Fertility verdict**: surprise density beats \*deru-, but topologically it's officialese — two classical fans, no native register contrast.

## *teḱs- / *tetḱ- 'to weave, fabricate, hew'

```
*teḱs-/*tetḱ- 'weave, fabricate, hew'   [? one root or two: Wiktionary lemmatizes *tetḱ- 'create, hew']
├─ Latin texere 'weave'                          [WEAVE arm]
│  ├─ textus 'thing woven' → text, context ('woven together'), pretext ('woven in front'), textile, texture
│  ├─ tēla 'web, warp'
│  │  ├─ subtilis 'finely woven' → subtle
│  │  ├─ French toile 'cloth' → toilet (cloth bag → dressing-cloth → grooming → room → fixture, 1895), toil (n. 'snare')
│  │  └─ ? Med. Latin telarium 'weaver's beam' → tiller
│  ├─ Old French tistre → tissue (woven band → fine cloth → anatomy, 18c.)
│  └─ ? testa 'pot' → test  [de Vaan: "unlikely for semantic reasons", probably loanword]
├─ Greek                                          [BUILD/CRAFT arm]
│  ├─ τέκτων 'builder, carpenter' → architect ('chief builder'), tectonic (→ plate tectonics)
│  └─ τέχνη 'craft, skill' → technology, technique, technical, polytechnic
├─ Sanskrit takṣan 'carpenter', Avestan taša 'axe', Hittite takš- 'join, build'  [HEW arm, no English]
├─ OCS tesla 'adze' → the Slavic surname Tesla ('the hewing tool' — and the car brand)
└─ ✗ PGmc *þahsuz 'badger' ("the builder") — REJECTED by current scholarship (substrate loan);
   dachshund is a debunking node, not a member
```

**Topology.** ~18 curated nodes; two fat arms carry all the English (Latin weave-cluster ~9, Greek build/craft ~6). **No native Germanic survivor — the exact inverse of \*deru-**: everything English is borrowed.

**Hook**: text, toilet, and technology are the same word — what you weave, what you build, and what you craft.

**Fertility verdict**: more hidden-connection punch per node than \*deru- (the toilet drift chain is a showstopper), but small and native-less; essay-or-medium-scale rather than grand-chart-scale.

## *lewk- 'light, bright; to shine'

```
*lewk- 'light, bright; to shine; to see'
├── *lewk-to- → PGmc *leuhtą → OE lēoht → light, lightning
├── *lówk-o-s 'light place, clearing'
│   ├── PGmc *lauhaz → OE lēah 'clearing' → lea; the -ley/-leigh place-names (Berkeley, Waterloo, surnames Lee/Leigh)
│   ├── Latin lūcus 'grove' (context)
│   └── Skt loká 'open space → WORLD' (context)
├── Latin lūx / lūcēre 'shine'
│   ├── lucid, lucent, elucidate, translucent, pellucid
│   ├── Lucifer 'light-bearer' — the morning star (the -fer half is *bʰer-)
│   ├── lux (the SI unit), lucubrate ('work by lamplight'), Lucy/Lucia
│   ├── ? lucerna 'lamp' → lucerne (alfalfa, via Provençal 'glowworm' — shiny seeds)
│   └── ? lustrāre 'light up' → illustrate, illustrious, luster  [or ← lustrum 'purification', three-way dispute]
├── *léwk-s-mn̥ → Latin lūmen
│   └── lumen (the other SI unit), luminous, illuminate, luminary, limn (via manuscript illumination)
├── *lówk-s-neh₂ 'THE SHINING ONE' → Latin lūna
│   ├── lunar, Luna, lunatic (moon-madness), lunette, sublunary
│   └── Proto-Slavic *luna → Russian луна; Armenian lusin (context)
├── Greek λευκός 'white' → leukemia, leukocyte, leuko-
│   ├── λύχνος 'lamp' → ? link (the torch; distinct from chain-link)
│   └── ? λύγξ → lynx ('the bright-eyed one'? — phonetic problems; Beekes: substrate)
├── Hittite lukk-ezzi 'is bright' (oldest attestation)
└── Skt rócate 'shines'; Welsh golau; Russian луч 'ray' (context)
```

**Topology.** ~35–40 English + ~10 context nodes. Unusual shape: not one trunk but a **Latin fan** — lūx, lūmen, lūna, lūstrāre are each mini-trees — plus one homely Germanic arm (light, lea) and one Greek (white).

**Hook — and the star pairing**: \*h₂stḗr names the glowing THING; \*lewk- names the GLOW. Zero node overlap, perfect thematic complement: the star chart owns the stars, \*lewk- owns the Moon (lūna 'the shining one', hence *lunatic*) and the morning star (Lucifer). Same night sky, same palette, complementary semantics — the natural companion piece to star-of-star. The lea/-ley place-names ('light place in the woods') are its *soot*-grade hidden gem, and both SI units of light (lux, lumen) are raw Latin from this root.
