title: Same word, unrecognizable
focus: [hit-hasterz, cy-seren, xcl-astl, txb-scirye, sa-tara]

Regular sound change is a slow disguise. Hittite **ḫašterz**, Welsh **seren**, Armenian **astł**, Tocharian **ścirye**, Sanskrit **tā́rā** — none of them sounds like *star*, and every one is the same word, each run through its own branch’s regular changes. Sanskrit even dropped the *s-* inside its own recorded history, in a Vedic contraction. The arms of this star are disguises.
