title: The star in your eye
focus: [sa-tara, la-stella, stellionate, stellarator]
---

Sanskrit **tā́rā** and Latin **stēlla** both came to mean the *pupil of the eye* — a little star that looks back at you. Latin **stelliō**, the star-spotted gecko, became a byword for a swindler, and **stellionate** — fraud with no more specific name — still stands in Scots law. And the **stellarator** is a machine built to do what stars do.
