title: The strangest paths
focus: [sa-tara, la-stella, stellionate, stellarator]
---

Where did the star end up with no sky in sight? In the **pupil of the eye**: Sanskrit *tā́rā* and Latin *stēlla* both grew that meaning — the little star that looks back at you. In court: Latin *stelliō*, the star-spotted gecko, became a word for a knave, and **stellionate** — fraud without a more special name — is still on the books in Scots law. And in the lab: the **stellarator** is a machine built to do what stars do.
