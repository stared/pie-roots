title: Latin’s other star
focus: []
---

Latin had a second star word, unrelated to *stēlla*: **sīdus**, *constellation*. English inherited it in hiding — to **consider** is, perhaps, *to examine the stars*; to **desire**, *to await what they will bring*. A real star in each, just not from this chart’s root. And Balto-Slavic named the same sky with a third word entirely: Polish **gwiazda**, Russian **звезда [zvezdá]**.
