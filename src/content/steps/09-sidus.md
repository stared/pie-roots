title: Hidden constellation, wrong root

The inverse surprise exists too. **Desire** and **consider** hide a star-meaning — Latin *sīdus*, ‘constellation’ (*dē-sīderāre*, perhaps ‘to await what the stars bring’; *cōn-sīderāre*, ‘to examine the stars’) — but *sīdus* is a different word entirely, no relation to *stēlla*. Latin had two star words; English inherited hidden pieces of both. And Balto-Slavic looked at the same sky and chose neither: Polish *gwiazda*, Russian *zvezda* — a root of its own.
